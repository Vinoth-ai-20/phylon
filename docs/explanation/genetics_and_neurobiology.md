# Genetics & Neurobiology

Phylon implements a state-of-the-art neuro-evolutionary pipeline combining explicit body plans with evolving neural networks.

## Hox Sequences (Morphology)

An organism's physical layout is defined by its HoxSequence.
- A sequence is an ordered list of HoxGene segments (e.g., Head, Torso, Tail).
- The growth_system reads these genes sequentially during the organism's zygote phase, spawning the physical particle nodes and connecting them with joints.
- Specific segments dictate biological properties (e.g., fins generate ctuation_amplitude).

## Compositional Pattern Producing Networks (CPPN)

While the morphology is guided by Hox genes, the brain's wiring is generated by a CPPN (a specialized neural network stored in the Genome).
- The CPPN operates as a blueprint. It maps spatial coordinates (the relative positions of two biological segments) to a connection weight.
- When an organism is born, its CPPN is queried for every possible pair of nodes. If the CPPN outputs a non-zero value, a synapse is created.
- This allows evolution to encode complex, repeating geometric symmetries (like bilateral walking gaits) efficiently.

## Continuous-Time Recurrent Neural Networks (CTRNN)

The resulting brain is a CTRNN. 
- Sensory inputs (Vision, Smell, Central Pattern Generator clocks) feed into the input layer.
- Activation flows through hidden layers governed by differential equations (time constants), allowing the brain to maintain short-term memory.
- Outputs dictate the contraction and expansion of muscle springs, driving the organism's physical locomotion in the environment.

# Phylon Biology Reference

This document serves as the ground-truth technical specification for the biological systems currently implemented in Phylon. It maps the scientific model described in the foundational design documents directly to their code implementations.

## 1. Energy Flow (Thermodynamics)

The ecosystem is fundamentally driven by sunlight and the conservation of mass/energy.

- **Sunlight**: The `GlobalAtmosphere` resource models a day/night cycle via a continuous cosine wave (60s period). Sunlight intensity determines the maximum rate of photosynthesis for producers.
- **Biomass**: All organisms have an abstract `mass` (sum of physical nodes).
- **Basal Metabolic Rate**: Energy expenditure scales super-linearly with mass ($M^{1.2}$) following Kleiber's Law. Larger organisms are exponentially more expensive to keep alive. If sunlight or CO₂ is low, Producers enter metabolic dormancy, reducing their basal cost by 80%.

*(Implemented in `crates/metabolism/src/lib.rs`)*

## 2. Chemical Economy

Organisms maintain a discrete internal chemical ledger (`ChemicalEconomy` component).

- **Glucose ($G$)**: Stored caloric energy.
- **Oxygen ($O_2$) & Carbon Dioxide ($CO_2$)**: Dissolved gases for respiration/photosynthesis.
- **ATP**: Immediately usable energy.

**Respiration**:
$$ 1\text{ Glucose} + 2\text{ O}_2 \rightarrow 5\text{ ATP} + 2\text{ CO}_2 $$
If ATP drops to $\le 0.0$, the organism dies (`Dead` marker).

**Photosynthesis** (Producers only):
$$ 1\text{ CO}_2 + \text{Sunlight} \rightarrow 1\text{ Glucose} + 1\text{ O}_2 $$

*(Implemented in `crates/metabolism/src/lib.rs` and `crates/ecology/src/lib.rs`)*

## 3. Trophic Dynamics (The Food Web)

The Phylon ecosystem is fully cyclical. Materials flow through a strict loop without arbitrary generation or deletion.

1. **Producers**: Convert environmental $CO_2$ and Sunlight into Glucose.
2. **Herbivores**: Consume fallen `FoodPellets` generated by Producers.
3. **Carnivores**: Continuously extract ATP and Glucose from living prey during physical collisions. A lethal strike drains ATP to $0.0$, naturally killing the prey.
4. **Decomposers**: Consume `Corpse` entities left behind by dead organisms.
5. **Recycling**: Decomposers excrete `MineralPellets`, which are absorbed by Producers to synthesize structural proteins for growth.

*(Implemented in `crates/ecology/src/lib.rs`)*

## 4. Neural Inputs (Sensory Ecology)

The brain (CTRNN) receives exactly 15 normalized continuous inputs $[-1.0, 1.0]$. The sensory system relies on spatial PDEs (diffusion fields) and direct physical raycasting.

### Internal State (Proprioception)
- `[0] ATP`: Current / Max
- `[1] Age`: Current / Max Lifespan
- `[2] Pacemaker`: Internal CPG oscillating at ~2Hz.

### Chemical Sensing (Olfaction)
- `[3] O₂ Gradient`: Sampled from the environment grid.
- `[4] CO₂ Gradient`: Sampled from the environment grid.
- `[5] Pheromones`: Signal field from other organisms.

### Environment
- `[6] Sunlight`: Direct atmospheric reading.
- `[7] Hazards`: Proximity to catastrophe zones.
- `[8] World Boundary`: Distance to the physical edge of the simulation.

### Vision (Organisms & Resources)
Vision is calculated by partitioning a conical FOV relative to velocity into 3 bins (Left, Center, Right). Signal strength is $1.0 - (d/range)$.
- `[9, 10, 11] Organism Vision`: Detecting organisms of predator/prey species.
- `[12, 13, 14] Resource Vision`: Diet-dependent detection (Minerals for Producers, Food for Herbivores, Corpses for Decomposers).

*(Implemented in `crates/sensing/src/lib.rs`)*

## 5. Morphogenesis (Development)

Organisms grow physically over their lifespan rather than spawning fully formed.

1. **Genome**: Organisms possess a `Genome` (currently `schema_version = 3`) containing a linear `HoxSequence`.
2. **Sequential Expression**: The `growth_system` reads the Hox sequence one segment at a time.
3. **Physical realization**: Each expressed segment spawns a physical `ParticleNode` attached by a `Spring` to the parent node.
4. **Differentiation**:
   - `Head`: Houses the brain, controls vision/olfaction.
   - `Muscle`: Translates neural output (CPPN channel 4) into physical actuation (spring contraction/expansion).
   - `Tail/Fin`: Provides passive drag or structural support.
5. **Symmetry**: A bifurcating Hox gene can branch the spine structure.

*(Implemented in `crates/organisms/src/systems.rs`)*

## 6. Evolutionary Cycle

The evolutionary loop captures genomes that successfully survive to reproductive age.
- **Lineage Tracking**: A global tracker monitors birth/death events to construct evolutionary trees.
- **Future Hooks**: The architecture is prepared for sexual reproduction, recombination, and continuous open-ended mutation, which will be integrated in Phase 8.

*(Implemented in `crates/evolution/src/lib.rs`)*

## 7. Future Horizons (Planned Interfaces)

The engine architecture allows for continuous expansion. Planned biological features include:
- Immune Response & Wound Healing
- Regeneration (Re-triggering Hox cascades)
- Hormonal state modifiers (Adrenaline, Melatonin)
- Developmental Plasticity (Environmentally triggered gene expression)
